about this site

The purpose of this web site is to communicate with the White Station High School - Class of 1970 in Memphis, Tennessee. Learn some interesting facts about White Station High School in wikipedia. We primarily use the website to share news, such as about reunions and other gatherings that we might have. The planning committee for class events includes Vanden Willey, Linda (Kantor) Goodwin-Parkinson, Bob Plunk, Sharon (Pepper) Goldstein, Cindy (Anderson) McInntyre, Randy Stepherson, Barby (Fooks) Perlberg, Beverly (Ball) Garrison, Debbie Robinson, Gail (Sacks) Kirshner, Kathy (Jobe) Hays, Leslie Ballin, Stephanie (Solomon) Brannon, Pege (Sternberger) Vaughan, Jimmy Lewis, Brad Hanover, and Steve Fergus. If you are a classmate who wants to join the committee or just receive information about our events, let us know.

Special Request to Class of 1970 (November, 2017)


Note to Classmates: The following email was sent to me (Linda Kantor) on behalf of our class; our planning committee requested that I post it here for you to see. After you read it -- If we have your current email address, you have heard from us about this opportunity to become involved. If we do not have your current email address, please click here to send it so that we can get the materials to you if you are interested in participating.

 

"We're a group of White Station parents working with Vice Principal Carrye Holland on a project to improve White Station. We're also working with a couple of key classes, particularly the Class of 1971. Ed Barnett, who I've cc'd, and my partner, Charlie Hill, suggested I write you. We need some help from the class of 1970. Take a look at the photographs. You remember our 'prison yard'. We received a small grant to help us turn it into a 'pocket park'. We hired a landscape architect who drafted the concept plan which turns the prison yard into...well, you can see. We're raising the money privately, holding it in a 501c3, and flintco is going to build it. We just started fundraising and we have almost half of the $180,000 we need. Can your class help us raise $10k as a 'sponsor' of our 'community courtyard'? Our goal is to have our pocket park built and growing by spring. Contact me if you can help.

Thanks,"

Richard J. Myers

Rush W. Siler (1917 – 2016)


Rush W. Siler, 99, died peacefully in his sleep early April 26, 2016 in Lexington, TN after a short illness. He was a resident of Regency Retirement Village in Jackson. Except for his service in the Pacific Theater in the Army Air Corp during World War II, Mr. Siler was a lifelong resident of this state. A Memphis educator, he taught at East High School and then became the first and long-serving principal of White Station High School, from 1951-1971. Read more -> The Jackson Sun on Apr. 29, 2016
